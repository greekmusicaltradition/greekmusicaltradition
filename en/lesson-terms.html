Understanding Our Terms & Conditions

Last Updated: July 18, 2025

Welcome to your music learning journey! To ensure clarity and a positive experience for all students, the following policies lay out how lessons are scheduled, conducted, and managed. Please take a moment to review each section carefully. By booking and attending lessons, students agree to abide by these policies.

Lesson Scheduling

  • Weekly slots are our standard lesson offering, providing consistent progress.
  • On-demand flexible bookings are available for those needing more flexibility, offered at a slightly higher rate.
  • All bookings (whether weekly or on-demand) must be made at least 24 hours in advance.
  • Specific teaching days and available hours are conveniently listed on our booking application.

Lesson Duration and Format

  • Each private lesson is precisely 45 minutes in duration, ensuring focused and productive sessions.
  • Lessons are offered both in-person at our studio and online via video conferencing, providing flexibility to suit your needs.

Attendance and Punctuality

  • Students may arrive up to 5 minutes late; however, please note the lesson will still conclude at the pre-arranged time (maximum 5 minutes past scheduled end).
  • Time lost due to late arrival cannot be made up or credited for future lessons.
  • We kindly ask all students to be prepared and ready to begin promptly at their scheduled lesson time.

Cancellations, Rescheduling, and Make-up Lessons

  • A minimum of 24 hours' notice is required for any lesson cancellation or rescheduling request. This applies to both weekly and on-demand lessons.
  • For Regular Weekly Students: You are permitted up to 4 reschedules per quarter (8 per year). Excessive rescheduling may lead to a review of this policy.
  • For On-demand Lessons: For every 5 lessons attended, you accrue 1 make-up lesson allowance.
  • If less than 24 hours' notice is given for a cancellation, the lesson will be forfeited and no refund will be issued.
  • Each student (both weekly and on-demand) is granted 2 late cancellation exemptions per year, which will be granted at the teacher's sole discretion for unforeseen circumstances.
  • Make-up lessons should ideally be scheduled and completed within the current payment period.

Payment Terms

  • All lessons must be prepaid. This applies to the entire series for weekly students or individually for on-demand lessons, before the first lesson takes place.
  • Accepted payment methods include credit card and PayPal. Additional payment options may be introduced in the future.
  • Our current lesson rates are accessible via the booking system, or you can request them directly.
  • Please note that lessons will not commence until payment has been successfully received.

Trial Lessons

  • A special trial month package is available, consisting of 4 weekly lessons at a discounted introductory price.
  • Payments for trial lessons are non-refundable.
  • There is no obligation or commitment to continue lessons or reserve the same time slot after the trial month concludes.
  • Each prospective student is eligible for one trial period only.

Materials and Equipment

  • All necessary lesson materials (e.g., sheet music, exercises) are provided digitally (e.g., as PDFs, audio files).
  • Students are responsible for printing materials if they prefer a physical copy; no physical delivery of materials will be made.
  • Unless specifically noted, no musical instrument is required for initial lessons.

Practice Expectations

  • It is highly recommended that students dedicate at least 2 hours per week to practice outside of lesson time to maximize progress.
  • For students under 14 years of age, we advise at least 1 hour weekly of practice, ideally with parental supervision to support their learning.
  • These are recommendations designed to foster your growth and enhance your enjoyment of learning music.

Communication

  • Our primary method of contact for scheduling, questions, and general communication is via messaging applications.
  • Phone calls can be arranged by mutual agreement after initial contact via messaging.
  • The teacher will respond to inquiries as promptly as possible, prioritizing urgency. While we strive for quick replies, there is no guaranteed response time.

Studio and Lesson Conduct

  • To maintain a focused learning environment, we request no eating and no distractions (e.g., phones muted, other tabs closed for online lessons) during lessons.
  • Students should arrive prepared for their lesson. Online students should ensure they are in a quiet and focused environment.
  • For in-person lessons, no guests (friends or siblings) are permitted to sit in during lesson times.
  • Parental Presence:
    • Online Lessons: Parents/guardians are welcome to be present for students under 14 years old, primarily to provide technical assistance if needed.
    • In-person Lessons: Parents are welcome. However, they may be asked to wait in the living room area unless their presence is specifically needed for the child's comfort or for technical support.

Holidays and Teacher Leave

  • Lessons will not be held during official school breaks (Christmas, Easter, and the annual summer break). These periods are planned in advance and will be communicated to students yearly.
  • Students will not be charged for lessons that do not take place during official holidays or pre-announced teacher leave.

Lesson Termination or Withdrawal

  • Monthly prepaid students are responsible for the entire month's payment. They may choose to attend any remaining lessons after providing notice of withdrawal, but no refunds will be issued for partial months.
  • On-demand students may discontinue lessons at any time, but we kindly request a brief notice for administrative purposes.
  • If the teacher cancels a lesson, a rescheduled lesson will be offered at a mutually convenient time. If rescheduling is not possible, a full refund for that specific lesson will be issued.

Privacy and Media Policy

  • All lesson content and personal student information are treated with the utmost privacy and confidentiality.
  • Progress updates may be shared with parents/guardians of students under 18 years of age.
  • Students are responsible for ensuring the privacy and security of their own devices and email accounts used for lessons.
  • Written permission will always be explicitly sought before using any lesson recordings, images, or testimonials for marketing or promotional purposes.

Liability and Safety

  • The teacher is not responsible for the loss or damage to personal property brought to the studio, or for accidents resulting from a student's own actions or negligence.
  • Our learning environment is regularly maintained to be safe and free of hazards, with any issues addressed promptly.
  • The teacher's liability is limited to incidents directly related to professional negligence.

Questions about our policy? We're here to help! Contact us at any time for clarification or to discuss special circumstances.
