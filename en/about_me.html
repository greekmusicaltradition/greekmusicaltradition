About Greek Traditional & Byzantine Music

About Me

Hello, I'm Olga — a music teacher specializing in Byzantine and Greek traditional singing.

I was born in Melbourne, Australia, and moved to Greece with my family when I was 8 years old. Growing up between cultures gave me a deep emotional connection to Greeks living abroad. I understand how meaningful music can be in maintaining our cultural roots and emotional identity — especially for those far from their homeland.

My Musical Journey

My musical journey began at the Experimental Music High School of Pallini, but it wasn't until 2012, through traditional Greek dance, that I reconnected deeply with music. That experience led me to join traditional music choirs, dive into study once again, and follow a path that brought me to the heart of this living tradition.

I hold a Diploma in Byzantine Music from the Music School of the Holy Metropolis of Mesogaia and Lavreotiki (Rafina branch), and a Degree in Greek Traditional Singing from the Protoporia Conservatory, where I studied under the guidance of renowned musician Christos Tsiaboulis.

My Teaching

Today, I teach both adults and children, in Greek and English, through online private lessons. As a native Greek speaker with strong command of English, I am especially passionate about helping members of the Greek diaspora improve their vocal technique and pronunciation. At the same time, I warmly welcome students living in Greece who feel drawn to this music — whether for cultural enrichment, performance, or personal growth.

I'm also deeply committed to teaching children. I believe that Greek traditional music offers beautiful imagery, grounding emotion, and a sense of inner balance that young people truly need in today's world. I see this music not just as art, but as a path to a more centered, soulful life.

My Approach

My teaching style is personal, respectful, and rooted in tradition. Whether you're just beginning or seeking to go deeper, I would be honoured to walk with you on your musical journey.
