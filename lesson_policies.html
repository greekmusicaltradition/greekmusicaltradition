Music Lesson Policies

Document Version: 1.0
Date: July 18, 2025
Document Type: Student Policy & Terms of Service

Introduction

Welcome to your music learning journey! To ensure clarity and a positive experience for all students, the following policies lay out how lessons are scheduled, conducted, and managed. Please take the time to review each section.

1. Lesson Scheduling

  • Weekly slots are the standard lesson offering.
  • On-demand flexible bookings are available at a higher rate.
  • All bookings (weekly or on-demand) must be made at least 24 hours in advance.
  • Specific teaching days and hours are listed on the booking app.

2. Lesson Duration and Format

  • Each lesson lasts 45 minutes.
  • Lessons are offered both in-person and online.

3. Attendance and Punctuality

  • Students may arrive up to 5 minutes late; however, the lesson still ends at the pre-arranged time (maximum 5 minutes past scheduled end).
  • Time lost due to late arrival is not made up or credited.
  • Please be prepared and ready to begin promptly.

4. Cancellations, Rescheduling, and Make-up Lessons

  • 24-hour notice is required for any cancellation or rescheduling (applies to both weekly and on-demand lessons).
  • Regular weekly students: Up to 4 reschedules per quarter (8 per year) are allowed. If excessive, this policy may be revised.
  • On-demand lessons: Each 5 lessons attended grants 1 make-up allowance.
  • If less than 24 hours' notice is given, the lesson is forfeited and not refunded.
  • Each student (both weekly and on-demand) may take 2 late cancellation exemptions per year, to be granted at the teacher's discretion.
  • Make-up lessons should be scheduled within the current payment period, when possible.

5. Payment Terms

  • All lessons must be prepaid before the first lesson in a series or individually for on-demand lessons.
  • Accepted payment methods include credit card and PayPal (additional methods may be added).
  • Rates are available via the booking system or upon request.
  • No lessons will take place unless payment has been received.

6. Trial Lessons

  • A trial month is available, consisting of 4 weekly lessons at a discounted price.
  • Trial payments are non-refundable.
  • No requirement or commitment to continue lessons or reserve the same time slot after the trial.
  • Each student is eligible for one trial period.

7. Materials and Equipment

  • All lesson materials are provided digitally (e.g., PDFs, audio).
  • Students are responsible for printing, if desired; no physical delivery of materials.
  • No instrument is required unless specified.

8. Practice Expectations

  • It is recommended that students practice at least 2 hours per week.
  • For students under 14 years of age, at least 1 hour weekly of practice with parental supervision is advised.
  • These are recommendations aimed at supporting your progress and enjoyment.

9. Communication

  • Primary contact is via messaging apps.
  • Phone calls are available by arrangement after initial messaging.
  • The teacher will respond as soon as possible according to urgency; no guaranteed response time.

10. Studio and Lesson Conduct

  • No eating and no distractions (e.g., phones muted) during lessons.
  • Students should arrive prepared; online students should ensure a quiet and focused environment.
  • No guests (friends or siblings) are permitted during lesson times.
  • Parental Presence:
    • Online: Parents/guardians may be present for students under 14 years old to provide technical help.
    • In-person: Parents are welcome, but may be asked to wait in the living room unless their presence is needed for the child's comfort or technical assistance.

11. Holidays and Teacher Leave

  • No lessons during official school breaks (Christmas, Easter, and annual summer break); these periods are planned in advance and will be communicated yearly.
  • Students will not be charged for lessons that do not take place during official holidays or teacher leave.

12. Lesson Termination or Withdrawal

  • Monthly prepaid students must pay for the entire month; they may choose whether to attend remaining lessons after providing notice of withdrawal.
  • On-demand students may discontinue lessons at any time but are kindly asked to provide notice.
  • If the teacher cancels a lesson, a rescheduled lesson will be offered after consulting with the student; if rescheduling is not possible, a refund will be issued.

13. Privacy and Media Policy

  • All lesson content and student information are treated as private and confidential.
  • Updates may be shared with parents/guardians of students under 18.
  • Students are responsible for the privacy and security of their own devices and email accounts.
  • Written permission will always be sought before using lesson recordings or images for marketing purposes.

14. Liability and Safety

  • The teacher is not responsible for loss or damage to personal property, or accidents resulting from a student's own actions.
  • The learning environment is kept safe and hazards are addressed promptly.
  • The teacher remains responsible for incidents related to negligence only.

Contact Information

If you have any questions, wish to discuss any special circumstances, or need clarification on any part of these policies, please reach out at any time via the preferred messaging channel.
